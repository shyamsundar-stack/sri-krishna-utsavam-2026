# Sri Krishna Utsavam 2026

**Register:** brand. This is a landing page for a festival. The design is the product.

## What it is

A one-page invitation and programme for the 16th Sri Krishna Utsavam, a devotional
Indian classical-arts festival held 31 July to 5 August 2026 at Bharatiya Vidya Bhavan,
Mylapore, Chennai. Six days of Carnatic vocal concerts, Bharatanatyam, Odissi and
Mohiniyattam recitals, and Sanskrit discourse. Entry is free. Every session is streamed.

Presented by the Sri Vishnu Mohan Foundation and Sri Gnana Advaitha Peetam, two
Chennai not-for-profits.

## Who it is for

1. **Chennai rasikas and families.** Many over 50. Often on mid or low-end Android over
   patchy mobile data. They want to know who is performing, when, and how to get there.
2. **The diaspora.** Watching the livestream from other timezones. They need the
   countdown to be correct for where they are, and the stream to be one tap away.
3. **People who forward things.** This audience shares on WhatsApp constantly. The
   printed invitation cards are the unit of sharing, not the URL.

## What success looks like

- Someone lands, learns the dates and venue, and finds their artist inside ten seconds.
- Someone forwards a specific artist's card to a friend without leaving the page.
- Nobody has to ask "is there a ticket" or "how do I watch from abroad".

## Jobs the page does

| Job | Where |
|---|---|
| Establish what and when | hero |
| Explain why this exists | about |
| Answer "who is on, and when" | schedule |
| Put faces to the names | artists |
| Get remote viewers to the stream | watch |
| Get local viewers to the hall | visit |
| Let anyone forward an invitation | share sheet on the poster strip |

## Constraints

- **Brand is fixed.** Palette, wordmark, artwork and photography are all extracted from
  the client's printed invitation PDF. Not open to reinterpretation.
- **No build step.** Plain HTML, CSS and JS, handed to a non-technical client and
  deployed to GitHub Pages.
- **Content is fixed** by the printed invitation. Where the PDF contradicts itself, the
  Schedule of Events page wins, and the discrepancy is noted in the README.
- **Single theme.** No light/dark toggle. The maroon-and-cream alternation is the
  invitation's own colour-block device, not a theme switch.
